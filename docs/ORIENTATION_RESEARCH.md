## 1. What traditional tarot actually teaches

| Source | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| **Rider–Waite / Biddy Tarot** | Standard practice is **upright + reversed** per card. Reversed is **not** one universal rule (not always “opposite,” not always “bad”). |
| **Tarot.com** ([reversals](https://www.tarot.com/tarot/interpretation-reversals)) | Common models: opposite, increased/decreased energy, **delays/blocked energy**, **internalized energy** — chosen **per reading**, not one algorithm. |
| **Sideways / crossing** | **Not** RW core. Used by some readers: Celtic Cross “crossing” = obstacle; Courtney Weber ([sideways card](https://courtneyaweber.com/thursday-tarot-tutorials-the-sideways-card/)) = issue warranting attention, something **crossing** you, or unexpected help (clarified by suit). |
| **Quantum Tarot 4 poles** | **Extension** of the deck: vertical = upright/reversed (traditional). Lateral = **transverse + conjugate**, which must be **derived per card** from that card’s archetype, imagery, and reversed keyword family — **not** “transverse = wait, conjugate = wrong.” |

### Deck mapping (lateral poles)

- **Transverse:** This card as **crossing / liminal** energy — the obstacle or attention-point **while the card’s lesson is still unfolding sideways** (Celtic “crossing” + card-specific upright themes in half-light).
- **Conjugate:** This card’s **other lateral face** — a **named, active** crossing drawn from that card’s **traditional reversed / shadow keywords** or second crossing image (e.g. Fool: “not ready to leap” vs “reckless leap”; Moon: “illusion on the path” vs “confusion burying intuition”).

Partner draw inverts poles (upright↔reversed, transverse↔conjugate); **text must differ per pole and per card.**

3. **Transverse** — lateral A (cite logic)

5. **Quantum reframe** (one line)
6. **Authoring** in `majorTransverse.ts` / `majorConjugate.ts` / `majorContent.ts` (vertical only)

**Sources:** Biddy Fool upright/reversed; RW cliff, dog, knapsack.

| Pole | Traditional basis | Quantum Tarot lateral meaning |
|------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| Upright | Beginnings, innocence, leap of faith | Open measurement, first step |
| Reversed | Holding back **or** recklessness (two poles) | Collapsed vertical |
| **Transverse** | Biddy reversed: “not ready to birth,” standstill at edge; crossing = **new path blocked by hesitation** | Threshold: expedition not started, cliff uncrossed |
| **Conjugate** | Biddy reversed flipside: **reckless risk**, disregard of consequences; crossing = **trip by impulsive leap** | Wrong collapse: measure too soon off the cliff |

**Not:** generic “wrong flank.” **Yes:** Fool’s documented reversed split (hold back vs reckless).
